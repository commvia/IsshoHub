Chapter 1 — Easily Confused Signs and Road Markings

The area where foreign licence conversion (外免切替, gaimen kirikae) exam candidates lose the most marks is pairs of signs that look similar but mean different things. This chapter organises these easily confused pairs into comparison tables, with an identification tip for each pair.

1-1　Easily Confused Signs

一方通行

One-Way Traffic

左折可

Left Turn Permitted

Travel only in the direction of the arrow. Driving in the opposite direction is a violation.

Vehicles may turn left even when the signal ahead shows red or yellow, but must yield to pedestrians.

👉 These two signs look very similar. The key is the arrow colour:

　　Blue background + white arrow　＝　One-Way Traffic (一方通行)

　　White background + blue arrow　＝　Left Turn Permitted (左折可)

始まり／ここから

Start / From Here

終わり／ここまで

End / Until Here

区間内

Within Zone

Indicates the regulation takes effect from this point.

Indicates the regulation ends at this point.

Indicates the regulation applies throughout this entire section.

👉 Identification tip: "→ Start" arrow extends forward; "← End" arrow points back; "↔ Full Zone" arrows point both ways.

追越しのための右側部分はみ出し通行禁止

No crossing into right lane to overtake

追越し禁止

No overtaking

Overtaking itself is not prohibited, but you cannot cross into the right half of the road while doing so. Common on narrower roads where overtaking within your own lane is still sometimes possible.

Overtaking is completely prohibited, regardless of the method used.

❓ Common exam question: If you see the 追越しのための右側部分はみ出し通行禁止 sign, does that mean you cannot overtake at all?

✅ Answer: No. Overtaking is still permitted as long as you don't cross into the right half of the road.

最高速度

Maximum Speed Limit

最低速度

Minimum Speed Limit

You must not exceed this speed. For example, "50" means a maximum of 50 km/h.

You must not go below this speed. For example, "30" means a minimum of 30 km/h.

👉 Identification tip: Look at whether there is a line under the number on the sign. Line under number = minimum limit; No line under number = maximum limit.

二輪の自動車・原動機付自転車通行止め

No motorcycles or mopeds

大型／普通自動二輪車二人乗り通行禁止

No two-up riding on motorcycles

All two-wheeled vehicles — including large motorcycles (自動二輪車) and mopeds (原動機付自転車, gentsuki) — are prohibited.

Large motorcycles (大型自動二輪車) and standard motorcycles (普通自動二輪車) may not carry a pillion passenger. Motorcycles with a sidecar are exempt.

👉 Why doesn't the "No Two-Up Riding" sign cover mopeds (原付)? Because mopeds are already prohibited from carrying passengers at all — a further prohibition would be redundant.

二輪の自動車以外の自動車通行止め

No motor vehicles other than motorcycles

自動車専用

Motor vehicles only

Only two-wheeled motor vehicles (motorcycles) may use this road. Other motor vehicles — such as four-wheeled cars — are prohibited.

Expressways or motor vehicle-only roads. Pedestrians, bicycles, and mopeds (原付) are all prohibited.

👉 Both signs feature a motor vehicle image, but they have opposite meanings. Key point: 自動車専用 includes motorcycles — it does not mean "four-wheeled vehicles only".

車線数減少

Lane reduction ahead

幅員減少

Road narrows ahead

The number of lanes ahead decreases (e.g. two lanes merge into one).

The entire road ahead narrows, but the number of lanes does not change.

👉 Identification tip: Look at whether the sign has lane dividing lines drawn on it.

　　With dividing lines　＝　Lane count decreases (車線数減少)

　　Without dividing lines　＝　Overall road width narrows (幅員減少)

ロータリーあり

Roundabout ahead

環状の交差点における右回り通行

Clockwise travel in roundabout

There is a roundabout (ロータリー) ahead.

You must travel clockwise around this roundabout (環状交差点).

👉 Identification tip: Yellow = warning sign ("roundabout ahead"); Blue = instruction sign ("you must travel this way").

右〈左〉つづら折りあり

Winding road (hairpin bends) ahead

すべりやすい

Slippery road surface

There is a series of sharp hairpin bends on the road ahead — multiple turns in quick succession.

The road surface ahead has low friction and is prone to skidding.

👉 Identification tip: Check whether the sign shows a vehicle. Serpentine arrow only = road shape (bends); Vehicle shown in sign = road condition (slippery surface).

横断歩道

Pedestrian Crossing

学校、幼稚園、保育所等あり

School / Kindergarten / Nursery ahead

Simply indicates there is a pedestrian crossing (横断歩道, odan hodō) ahead. These signs come in various designs — not just children, but adults too.

Indicates a school, kindergarten, or nursery is nearby. Watch out for children who may suddenly run into the road.

👉 Identification tip: The blue sign has a zebra-crossing pattern. The yellow sign is a warning, alerting you to potential hazards ahead.

❓ Exam question: Does the blue sign indicate a school or kindergarten nearby?

✅ Answer: No. It indicates a pedestrian crossing (横断歩道).

1-2　Easily Confused Road Markings

駐停車禁止

No Stopping or Parking

駐車禁止

No Parking

You cannot stop here at all — not even briefly to drop off passengers or unload goods. (駐停車禁止 = no 駐車 parking + no 停車 stopping)

You cannot park for extended periods (駐車), but brief stops (停車) — such as waiting or dropping off passengers — are permitted.

👉 Check the roadside marking: A solid yellow line is the strongest prohibition — it means "no stopping or parking at all"; A dashed line or single-regulation marking usually means "no parking only".

安全地帯

Safety Zone

立ち入り禁止部分

No-Entry Area

停止禁止部分

No-Stopping Zone

Pedestrian-only area, such as a tram stop platform.

❌ Vehicles may not enter

❌ No parking

A marked area that vehicles may never enter under any circumstances.

❌ No entry

❌ No parking

Vehicles may pass through, but if you anticipate stopping within the zone (e.g. due to congestion or a red light ahead), you must not enter.

👉 Common rule: Whenever you see a yellow border, vehicles may not intrude.

👉 Blank interior = pedestrian safety zone (e.g. tram platform).

👉 Diagonal lines inside = prohibited zone.

Yellow lines on both sides of centre line

Neither direction may cross

Yellow line on one side of centre line only

One direction may not cross

Vehicles in either direction are prohibited from crossing into the oncoming lane to overtake.

Only vehicles on the yellow-line side may not cross; vehicles on the white-line side may still overtake.

👉 Key rule: Look at the colour of the centre line on your side of the road.

　　Yellow on your side　→　You cannot cross to overtake　　White on your side　→　You may cross to overtake

進路変更禁止 — Lane Change Rules

Yellow lines on both sides of lane marking

Neither side may change lanes

Yellow line on one side of lane marking only

One side may not change lanes

Neither lane A nor lane B may change to the other (cannot move from A to B, or from B to A).

Only the lane on the yellow-line side cannot change to the other side; the other side may change lanes freely.

👉 The logic is the same as for overtaking centre lines: yellow on your side = you cannot cross over.
