Chapter 13 — Driving Situation Rules

This chapter covers stand-alone but frequently tested topics: adverse weather driving, the two-stage right turn for mopeds (原付), and pillion passenger conditions for motorcycles.

13-1　Adverse Weather Driving

"Just drive slowly when the weather is bad" is not enough — each type of weather creates different hazards.

Weather

Hazard

Response

Rain 🌧️

Road surface becomes slippery; braking distance increases.

Asphalt is most slippery at the very start of rain.

Construction site steel plates and tram rails are also prone to skidding.

・Reduce speed and keep a longer following distance compared to dry conditions

・Take extra care when passing steel plates or rail tracks

Snow ❄️

Snow accumulation makes the surface unstable and reduces tyre grip.

・Fit anti-skid equipment (snow chains, etc.) and reduce speed

・Follow the tyre tracks (わだち, wadachi) left by the vehicle ahead where possible

Fog 🌫️

Visibility ahead is obscured by fog; safe sighting distance is greatly reduced.

・Switch on low-beam headlights (下向き灯) or fog lamps early and reduce speed

・Sound the horn when necessary to warn oncoming or leading vehicles

13-2　Two-Stage Right Turn for Mopeds (原付)

Mopeds (原動機付自転車, gentsuki) must use the "two-stage right turn" (二段階右折, nidankai usetsu) at certain intersections — unlike the right-turn method used by ordinary cars.

Right-Turn Method

Applicable Intersections

Two-Stage Right Turn

（二段階右折）

・Intersections with a "原動機付自転車の右折方法（二段階右折）" sign posted

・Intersections with traffic control signals where there are 3 or more lanes

Normal Right Turn (like a car)

（小回り）

・Intersections with a "原動機付自転車の右折方法（小回り）" sign posted

・Intersections with traffic control signals where there are 2 or fewer lanes

・Intersections without traffic control signals

💡 Why a two-stage right turn?

Mopeds travel more slowly than ordinary cars. If they cut across to the centre lane to turn right — as cars do — they risk being rear-ended by faster-moving vehicles behind them. Therefore, at intersections with many lanes (more lanes = more cars = greater speed differential), mopeds are required to use the two-stage method: first proceed straight across to the far side, then pivot to face right.

13-3　Pillion Passenger Conditions for Motorcycles

Road Type

Conditions for Carrying a Pillion Passenger

Ordinary roads

Hold a large motorcycle (大型二輪) or standard motorcycle (普通二輪) licence for 1 year or more

Expressways

Be aged 20 or over, and hold a large motorcycle (大型二輪) or standard motorcycle (普通二輪) licence for 3 years or more

🚫 Motorcycles that can never carry a pillion passenger

・Large motorcycles (大型自動二輪) or standard motorcycles (普通自動二輪) with no rear seat — rated for 1 person only, so carrying a passenger is not possible by design

・Mopeds (原動機付自転車, gentsuki) — carrying a pillion passenger is prohibited outright

💡 Why these restrictions?

Motorcycles are inherently less stable than four-wheeled vehicles; adding a pillion raises the centre of gravity and makes control harder at speed. The regulations therefore set thresholds across three dimensions — licence experience, road type, and vehicle design — to ensure the rider has sufficient skill.

🧠 Quick memory aid

Ordinary roads: 1 year licence | Expressways: aged 20 + 3 years licence.

Mopeds: never allowed. Motorcycles with no rear seat: never allowed.
