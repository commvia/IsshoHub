Chapter 9 — Signals, Gestures & Lighting Rules

Before turning, changing lanes, making a U-turn, stopping, or reversing, drivers must give a signal (合図, aizu) in advance to alert surrounding vehicles and pedestrians. The correct timing for each signal is a frequently tested topic in the exam.

9-1　Signal Timing and Method for Each Manoeuvre

The table below summarises all manoeuvres that require a signal. Row background colours are grouped into three sets: green = left-direction, red = right-direction, grey = slow/stop/reverse.

Manoeuvre

When to Signal

How to Signal

Turn Left

左折

30 m before the point where you begin to turn left.

Operate the left turn signal, or

Extend the right arm out of the vehicle with the elbow bent vertically upward, or

Extend the left arm out of the vehicle horizontally straight.

Exit a Roundabout

環状交差点を出るとき

When passing alongside the exit immediately before the one you intend to use.

※ Do not signal when entering a roundabout.

※ If exiting at the first exit after entering, signal at the moment of entry.

Operate the left turn signal (same as turning left).

Change Lane Left

進路変更（左）

3 seconds before making the lane change.

Operate the left turn signal (same as turning left).

Turn Right / U-Turn

右折・転回（U-turn）

30 m before the point where you begin to turn right or make a U-turn.

Operate the right turn signal, or

Extend the right arm out of the vehicle horizontally straight, or

Extend the left arm out of the vehicle with the elbow bent vertically upward.

Change Lane Right

進路変更（右）

3 seconds before making the lane change.

Operate the right turn signal (same as turning right).

Slow Down / Stop

徐行・停止

Signal as you do it.

Activate the brake lights, or

Extend an arm out of the vehicle and point it diagonally downward.

Reverse

後退

Signal as you do it.

Activate the reversing lights, or

Extend an arm out of the vehicle pointing diagonally downward, palm facing rearward, and swing the arm back and forth.

9-2　Quick-Reference: Signal Timing

🧠 Three Distance / Time Memory Groups

Manoeuvre Type

Timing

Why

Turning (right / left / U-turn)

30 m before

The turning point is fixed, so distance is the meaningful measure

Lane changes (left / right)

3 seconds before

The lane-change point is not fixed, so time is used instead

Slow down, stop, or reverse

Signal as you do it

These actions are brief; no advance warning is required

⭐ One-line mnemonic: "Turns: 30 m — Lane changes: 3 seconds — Slow / stop / reverse: signal immediately"

9-3　Special Rules for Roundabouts

Signalling rules for roundabouts (環状交差点) differ from regular intersections — memorise these separately:

🔸 No signal on entry　Do not use a turn signal when entering a roundabout.

🔸 Signal before your exit　When leaving a roundabout, signal as you pass alongside the exit immediately before the one you intend to use.

🔸 Exception　If you are exiting at the very first exit after entering, signal at the moment of entry (since there is no prior exit to use as a reference point).

9-4　Hand Gesture Reference

If a turn signal is broken, or when riding a bicycle, hand gestures must be used instead. Exam questions frequently test which gesture corresponds to which direction.

🧠 Memory tip: Think of the right arm as the "active" arm. To indicate left with the right arm, bend upward; to indicate right, extend straight. The left arm is the mirror opposite.

9-5　Lighting Requirements (灯火)

💡 Two Purposes of Vehicle Lighting

Before memorising each individual scenario, keep this concept in mind: lighting serves two purposes.

・For yourself → illuminates the road so you can see what is ahead.

・For others → makes your vehicle visible to other road users.

Lights used while driving are mainly for yourself; lights used while parked or stopped are mainly for others.

🧠 Key Distance Numbers

・Regular roads: 50 m (minimum visible distance while driving; also the threshold for parked-vehicle visibility)

・Expressways: 200 m (minimum visible distance while driving; also the visibility threshold for expressway parking/stopping warnings)
