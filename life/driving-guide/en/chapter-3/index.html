Chapter 3 — Road Marking Quick Reference

This chapter organises Japanese road markings (painted on the road surface) into a quick-reference table. Each marking includes its Japanese name, an illustration, and identification tips. Give it a quick read before your exam — and you'll be able to recognise them on the road too.

3-1　No-Entry & Restricted Zones

Marking Name

Illustration

Description & Key Points

Roadside Strip

路側帯

The shoulder area outside the white line. Pedestrians and light vehicles (e.g. bicycles) may use it; if the strip is wide enough, cars may also park there.

👉 A single white line = parking permitted; two lines means one of the two restricted variants below applies.

No-Parking / No-Stopping Roadside Strip

駐停車禁止路側帯

A roadside strip where cars are prohibited from parking or stopping. Pedestrians and light vehicles may still use it, but motor vehicles may not enter to park or stop.

👉 Identified by a dashed line alongside the white line.

Pedestrian-Only Roadside Strip

歩行者用路側帯

A roadside strip reserved exclusively for pedestrians. All vehicles are prohibited from entering and from parking.

👉 Marked with two solid lines — a stronger prohibition than the version above.

No-Stopping Zone

停止禁止部分

A box grid painted at intersections or junctions. Vehicles may not stop inside. If traffic ahead is congested or the light is red and you judge that you will end up stopped inside the box, you must not enter.

👉 Comparable to the yellow box junction in the UK or the box marking in Hong Kong.

End of Regulation

終わり

The preceding traffic regulation (e.g. speed limit, no-overtaking zone) ends here.

👉 Regulation signs are usually paired with an "終わり" marking to show where the restriction is lifted.

3-2　Lane Markings

Marking Name

Illustration

Description & Key Points

Lane Designation

車両通行帯

Vehicles must travel within the designated lane and may not cross the line arbitrarily.

👉 This is the fundamental meaning of lane markings; all subsequent rules build on this principle.

Main Lane Priority

優先本線車道

The main road ahead is a priority road; vehicles merging from a side lane must yield to traffic on the main lane.

👉 The lane marked "本線" (main lane) is the priority one.

Lane Allocation by Vehicle Type

車両通行区分

Different lanes are assigned to different vehicle types. The marking indicates which lane is designated for which vehicle (e.g. two-wheeled vehicles, light vehicles, motor vehicles).

👉 Commonly seen on multi-lane roads or expressways.

Designated Lane for Specific Vehicle Type

特定の種類の車両の通行区分

Certain vehicle types are required to use a specific lane. For example, large trucks must use the leftmost lane.

👉 The purpose is to concentrate heavy vehicles in one lane and avoid disrupting traffic flow.

Towing Vehicle Lane on National Expressway

牽引自動車の高速自動車国道通行区分

Towing vehicles (牽引自動車) must use the designated lane on national expressways.

👉 Typically the leftmost lane — similar to the rules for large vehicles.

Towing Vehicle Lane on Motor Vehicle Road

牽引自動車の自動車専用道路

A road designated for towing vehicles (牽引自動車).

👉 Separated from general lanes to improve the safety and flow of large vehicle traffic.

Lane Allocation by Direction

進行方向別通行区分

Before an intersection, select the lane that corresponds to your intended direction (straight ahead / left turn / right turn).

👉 When you see directional arrows on the road surface, move into the correct lane in advance.

Exclusive Lane

専用通行帯

Only the vehicles specified on the marking may use this lane — for example, a "Bus Only" lane. All other vehicles are prohibited from entering.

👉 Unlike the "Priority Lane" below, this lane is exclusive — no other vehicles may enter even temporarily.

Bus Priority Lane

路線バス等優先通行帯

Buses have priority, but other vehicles may also use the lane provided they do not obstruct buses.

👉 Key point: this is not a complete prohibition — buses simply have right of way.

3-3　Parking Methods

Marking Name

Illustration

Description & Key Points

Parallel Parking

平行駐車

Park with the vehicle body parallel to the kerb.

👉 The most common form of kerbside parking — vehicle faces the direction of travel.

Angle Parking

斜め駐車

Park with the vehicle body at an angle to the kerb.

👉 Easier to enter and exit than perpendicular parking; commonly seen on shopping streets or roadsides.

Perpendicular Parking

直角駐車

Park with the vehicle body at 90 degrees to the kerb.

👉 Commonly seen in car parks or designated roadside bays.

3-4　Pedestrian Crossings & Bicycle Markings

Marking Name

Illustration

Description & Key Points

Crossing Ahead Warning

横断歩道あり 自転車横断帯あり

Advance warning that a pedestrian crossing or bicycle crossing zone is ahead.

👉 When you see this, slow down and be prepared to yield to pedestrians or cyclists.

Pedestrian Crossing

横断歩道

The zebra crossing painted on the road surface. Vehicles must give way to pedestrians.

👉 Cyclists must in principle dismount and push their bike to count as a pedestrian.

Diagonal Crossing Permitted

斜め横断可

Pedestrians may cross diagonally across the intersection rather than following the right-angle edges. There are two versions: "time-limited" and "all-day".

👉 Most commonly found at scramble intersections (all-direction green phase, where pedestrians may cross in any direction).

Bicycle Crossing Zone

自転車横断帯

A dedicated crossing zone that allows cyclists to ride across the road without dismounting.

👉 Distinct from the pedestrian zebra crossing — pedestrians use the zebra stripes; cyclists use the zone marked with a bicycle symbol.

Bicycles Permitted on Pavement

普通自転車歩道通行可

Pavements marked with this symbol permit bicycle access.

👉 Bicycles should ordinarily use the roadway — this marking indicates an exceptional section where the pavement is permitted.

Bicycle Section on Pavement

普通自転車の歩道通行部分

A designated section of the pavement marked for bicycle use.

👉 Cyclists use the marked section; pedestrians use the rest — separating them to reduce conflict.

Bicycles Prohibited from Entering Intersection

普通自転車の交差点進入禁止

Bicycles are not permitted to enter this intersection.

👉 Typically found at large intersections to prevent conflicts between bicycles and motor vehicles.

3-6　Centre Lines & Lane Boundary Lines

Marking Name

Illustration

Description & Key Points

Centre Line

中央線

Marks the centre or centreline of the road.

Specifically indicates that crossing to the right side is prohibited.

Centre Line

中央線

② General road (where the special restriction above does not apply).

Centre Line

中央線

③ The centre line is not drawn at the physical centre of the road, but is positioned elsewhere as required.

Centre Line

中央線

④ Other cases where a centre line marking is specifically necessary. 👉 Solid yellow line = may not cross; White dashed line = may cross in principle

Lane Boundary Line

車線境界線

Marks lane boundaries on roads with four or more lanes in the same direction.

① Marked on the road surface using paint or similar material.

Lane Boundary Line

車線境界線

② Marked using cat's-eyes, stone, or similar materials.

👉 Both versions carry the same meaning; only the material differs. Commonly found on wide multi-lane roads.

3-7　Tram Stops, Safety Zones & Channelisation Markings

Marking Name

Illustration

Description & Key Points

Tram Stop

路面電車停留所

Marks the stop for a streetcar (tram).

👉 When a tram is stopped, motor vehicles must yield to passengers boarding and alighting.

Safety Zone

安全地帯

An area reserved for pedestrian safety, such as a tram boarding platform.

👉 Vehicles must never enter and must not park here. Take note whenever you see a yellow border.

Approaching Safety Zone / Road Obstruction

安全地帯または路上障害物に接近

Alerts drivers that a safety zone or road obstruction is ahead, requiring evasive action. There are two versions: "can only pass on one side" and "can pass on either side".

👉 Slow down and follow the arrow direction to navigate around the obstacle.

Channelisation Marking

導流帯

A section of road surface (usually a hatched area) marked off to guide traffic into safe, smooth paths — vehicles are prohibited from using this area.

👉 Commonly found at complex junctions or where lanes diverge near ramps. Neither entering nor parking is permitted.

Priority Road Ahead

前方優先道路

Advance warning that a priority road (main road) is ahead — you are on a side road and must yield to traffic on the main road.

👉 Usually appears together with a "徐行" (proceed slowly) or "止まれ" (stop) sign.

3-8　Turning Methods

Marking Name

Illustration

Description & Key Points

Right Turn Path Guide

右折の方法

When turning right at an intersection marked with this symbol, you must pass through the designated area before completing the turn.

👉 The purpose is to clarify the right-turn path at multi-lane intersections and prevent collisions.

Left Turn Path Guide

左折の方法

After turning left, you must proceed directly into the designated lane.

👉 You cannot freely change to another lane immediately after completing the turn.

Roundabout Turning Path

環状交差点における左折等の方法

Inside a roundabout, whether turning left, right, going straight, or making a U-turn, you must follow the designated path around the circle.

👉 Roundabouts travel anti-clockwise (one-way). Simply exit at the correct outlet.
