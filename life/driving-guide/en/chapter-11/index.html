Chapter 11 — Parking & Stopping Rules

Japanese traffic law draws a sharp distinction between "parking" (駐車, chūsha) and "stopping" (停車, teisha). Once you understand the difference, the prohibition rules become much easier to remember. This chapter covers three topics: the definitions, locations where only parking is prohibited, and locations where both parking and stopping are prohibited.

💡 One-line distinction

Both involve a stationary vehicle, but the key difference is readiness to move. "Stopping" (停車) means the vehicle can pull away immediately — it's brief and responsive. "Parking" (駐車) means it cannot move right away — it's prolonged or abandoned. The two dividing lines are: "5 minutes" and "can the vehicle move immediately?".

⭐ Memory aid: "1–3–5 Fire, Gate, Hose"

Link each number to its category in order: 1 m — fire alarm box; 3 m — vehicle entrance/exit gate; 5 m — construction zone and fire-fighting equipment (hose connections, hydrants, cisterns).
