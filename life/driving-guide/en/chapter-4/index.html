Chapter 4 — Traffic Signals & Police Gestures

Traffic lights may seem straightforward, but they are among the trickiest areas in the written exam — particularly the distinctions between "steady" and "flashing" lights, arrow signals, pedestrian signals, and police hand gestures.

4-1　Vehicle Signals: Steady vs Flashing

Japanese signals are not simply "on" or "off" — they also have a flashing (点滅, tenmetsu) state. The same colour can mean something completely different when flashing versus steady. This is a key exam trap.

Colour

Steady (点灯)

Flashing (点滅)

🟢 Green (青)

Proceed. Straight ahead, turn left, or turn right are all permitted.

(Not used in general practice)

🟡 Yellow

Stop in principle.

However, if you are already close to the intersection and stopping safely would itself be dangerous, you may continue through.

Proceed with caution — no obligation to stop.

There is no legal requirement to slow down; you are not required to crawl through.

🔴 Red

Stop. You must not pass the stop line.

Come to a complete stop, confirm it is safe, then proceed.

⚠️ Key Exam Traps

❓ "When the signal is yellow, vehicles and pedestrians may check for other traffic and proceed." Many people mark this ○, but the correct answer is ✗.

A steady yellow means "stop"; it is only a flashing yellow that means "proceed with caution". When reading exam questions, look carefully for the word 点滅 (flashing).

❓ "When the yellow signal is flashing, vehicles must pass slowly." This sounds reasonable, but the answer is ✗. A flashing yellow requires attention to traffic safety, but there is no requirement to travel slowly.

4-2　Arrow Signals

A green arrow appearing below a red signal means "vehicles may proceed in the direction of the arrow even though the main signal is red".

💡 Key Concept

Arrow signals take precedence over the main signal — even if the main signal is red, you may proceed in the direction the arrow indicates.

4-3　Pedestrian Signals

State

Meaning

🟢 Green figure — steady

Pedestrians may cross.

🟢 Green figure — flashing

Pedestrians already crossing may continue; pedestrians who have not yet started must not begin crossing.

🔴 Red figure — steady

Pedestrians must not enter the roadway. Anyone already in the roadway must cross quickly or return to where they started.

🚗 Note for drivers

When setting off on a green signal, check the pedestrian crossing for "remaining pedestrians" (残存歩行者 — people who were still crossing when the green figure started flashing), as well as pedestrians or cyclists who may suddenly enter. Only proceed once you have confirmed it is safe.

🧠 Memory Logic

・Traffic facing the officer's front → same as a red light facing you — stop.

・Traffic to the officer's side → same as a green light (when arms are horizontal).

・Arm raised upward → equivalent to yellow for all directions.

4-5　Signal Priority Order

If signals, road signs, and police gestures are all present at the same intersection at the same time, which takes precedence?

👮 Police gestures　＞　🚦 Traffic signals　＞　🪧 Road signs

Police gestures are live, on-the-spot direction — they carry the strongest authority. Signals and signs are pre-installed rules. When all three conflict, follow the officer's instruction.
