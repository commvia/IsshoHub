Chapter 6 — Vehicle Categories & Weight Rules

Japan's Road Traffic Act divides vehicles into several categories, each with different licence requirements, speed limits, and loading rules. There are four main groups: four-wheeled, two-wheeled, mopeds (原付), and small special vehicles. Getting the classification right is the foundation for understanding all the rules that follow.

6-6　Exam-Prone Details

📌 Four-wheeled vehicles — the vehicle inspection certificate (車検証) is the reference

The maximum payload for four-wheeled vehicles is not determined by the vehicle type — it is the figure recorded in the "vehicle inspection certificate (自動車検査証)" or "light vehicle notification certificate (軽自動車届出証)" for that individual vehicle. Exception: mini cars (ミニカー) have a fixed limit of 90 kg.

📌 Overhang rule — the "× 1.2" formula

For four-wheeled vehicles, cargo length and width may each reach up to 1.2 times the vehicle's own dimensions. This translates to a maximum overhang of 0.1 times the vehicle length (or width) at each end.

Example: A vehicle that is 5 m long may carry cargo up to 6 m long — with up to 0.5 m overhanging at the front and 0.5 m at the rear.

📌 Height — 3.8 m for most four-wheeled vehicles, only 2.5 m for light vehicles

Measured from the ground, the maximum total height including cargo for a standard four-wheeled vehicle is 3.8 m. However, three-wheeled ordinary vehicles and light vehicles with a total engine displacement of 660 cc or less are limited to 2.5 m.

📌 Two-wheeled vehicle exception — based on the carrier (裝載裝置) dimensions

For two-wheeled vehicles (excluding sidecars), measurements are based on the cargo carrier, not the vehicle body. Cargo may extend 0.3 m beyond the carrier length, and 0.15 m beyond each side of the carrier width. Maximum payload is 60 kg.
