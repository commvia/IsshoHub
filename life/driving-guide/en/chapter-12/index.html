Chapter 12 — Expressway Special Rules

Expressways have many special rules that do not apply to ordinary roads, covering how to enter and exit, following distances, what to do in a breakdown, and a list of absolute prohibitions. These rules deserve particular attention.

12-2　Entering and Exiting the Expressway

🛣️ Entering the expressway (merging)

When entering via the on-ramp (acceleration lane / 加速レーン kasoku rēn):

1. Accelerate on the acceleration lane to match the speed of the main-lane traffic flow.

2. Once the main lane is clear (or there is a sufficient gap), signal and merge in.

3. Main-lane vehicles have no obligation to give way to you — you are the one who must yield.

🛣️ Exiting the expressway (leaving)

1. Move to the far-left lane (exit lane) well in advance.

2. Only begin to slow down after you have entered the deceleration lane (減速レーン gensoku rēn).

3. Do not brake suddenly in the main lane — vehicles behind you may rear-end you.

12-3　Prohibited Actions on Expressways

Prohibited action

Reason

🚫 U-turn（Uターン）

U-turns are absolutely prohibited on expressways. If you miss your exit, drive to the next exit, leave, and re-enter going the other direction.

🚫 Wrong-way driving（逆行）

Absolutely prohibited — driving the wrong way is one of the most dangerous violations possible.

🚫 Crossing lanes to the opposite side（横断）

You cannot cut across from the middle of the roadway to the opposing carriageway.

🚫 Reversing（後退）

You cannot reverse back if you have missed your exit.

🚫 Driving on the hard shoulder / roadside strip

The hard shoulder may only be used temporarily in the event of a breakdown.

🚫 Parking on the hard shoulder (non-breakdown)

Stopping on the hard shoulder is only permitted in unavoidable circumstances such as a breakdown.

12-5　Following Distance (Gap to the Vehicle Ahead)

Expressway speeds are high — when the vehicle ahead brakes suddenly, you need adequate time to react. The legally recommended following distance increases with speed.

Speed

Recommended gap (dry road)

Recommended gap (wet / slippery road)

80 km/h

approx. 80 m

approx. 160 m (×2)

100 km/h

approx. 100 m

approx. 200 m (×2)

🧠 Quick memory rule

"Speed N km/h → gap N metres" — this is the basic rule for dry roads. In rain or on a slippery surface, allow double the distance.

12-6　What to Do in a Breakdown or Accident

A breakdown on an expressway is an extremely dangerous situation — vehicles behind you are travelling at high speed. The correct sequence of actions is critical.

📋 Standard breakdown SOP

Step

Action

1

Move the vehicle to the hard shoulder or an emergency stopping area as far as possible — avoid blocking a lane.

2

Switch on the hazard warning lights (双方向点滅 / double-flash).

3

Place a 停止表示器材 (warning triangle or light-emitting signal bar) at least 50 m behind the vehicle.

4

All occupants must exit the vehicle and move to a safe position beyond the guardrail — do not remain inside the vehicle.

5

Call #9910 (道路緊急ダイヤル Road Emergency Dial) or 110 to report the incident and wait for roadside assistance.

⚠️ Common exam mistakes

❓ "In the event of a breakdown, you should remain inside the vehicle and wait for rescue." — This is WRONG ✗.

Staying inside the vehicle is extremely dangerous because vehicles behind may collide into you. The correct action is for all occupants to exit and move to a safe position beyond the guardrail.

❓ "When visibility is below 200 m during the day, a stopped vehicle must also turn on its hazard lights or side marker / tail lights." — This is CORRECT ○. This is an expressway-specific rule — remember the 200 m figure.

12-7　Climbing Lanes and Overtaking Lanes

🏔️ 登坂車線 Tōhan shansen — Climbing lane (uphill auxiliary lane)

On steep sections, a 登坂車線 is provided for slow-moving vehicles (such as large lorries) so that faster vehicles can pass using the regular lane.

⚠️ Note: The climbing lane is not "compulsory for large vehicles" — it is simply available for any vehicle travelling slowly. Regular cars may also use it.

🏎️ 追越車線 Oikoshi shansen — Overtaking lane (far-right lane)

On multi-lane expressways, the far-right lane is the overtaking lane. In principle it may only be used for overtaking; after completing an overtake, you must return to the regular lane.

⚠️ Occupying the overtaking lane for an extended period — even if you are driving at the legal speed limit — is also a violation, known as 通行帯違反 (tsūkōtai ihan).
