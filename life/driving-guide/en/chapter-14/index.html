Chapter 14 — Driver Responsibilities & Prohibited Driving

Holding a licence is not just about driving skill — it comes with legal responsibility. This chapter covers Japan's Road Traffic Act requirements for driver condition, absolutely prohibited driving situations, documents you must carry, and statutory duties when an accident occurs.

14-1　Absolutely Prohibited Driving Situations

The following situations are absolute prohibitions. Violations carry severe penalties and may result in criminal liability.

Prohibited Situation

Standard

Severity of Penalty

🍺 Drink-driving 酒気帯び運転

Breath alcohol concentration ≥ 0.15 mg/L

Up to 3 years imprisonment or ¥500,000 fine

🍺 Drunk driving 酒酔い運転

Visibly incapable of driving normally

Up to 5 years imprisonment or ¥1,000,000 fine

💊 Driving under medication

Taking drugs that impair consciousness (including certain cold-medicine ingredients)

Treated the same as drunk driving

😴 Fatigued driving

Excessive fatigue, sleep deprivation, illness, or similar impaired states

Up to 3 years imprisonment or ¥500,000 fine

📱 Using a mobile phone while driving

Calling, looking at the screen, messaging, etc.

Up to 6 months imprisonment or ¥100,000 fine

🚗 Driving without a valid licence

No valid licence, licence suspended, or wrong licence category for the vehicle

Up to 3 years imprisonment or ¥500,000 fine

⚠️ Shared Liability

Japan enforces strict shared liability for drink-driving offences. The following people are also subject to penalties:

・ Knowingly providing alcohol to someone who is about to drive (offence of supplying alcohol — 酒類提供罪)

・ Knowingly riding as a passenger in a vehicle driven by a drunk driver (offence of riding along — 同乗罪)

・ Knowingly lending a vehicle to someone about to drink-drive (offence of providing a vehicle — 車両提供罪)

14-2　Documents Required While Driving

Item

Details

📇 Driver's licence 運転免許証

Must be carried at all times while driving. Driving without it — even if you hold a valid licence — constitutes 免許不携帯 (failure to carry licence) and is subject to a fine.

📄 Vehicle inspection certificate 車検証（自動車検査証）

Must be kept in the vehicle at all times (not carried by the driver personally). Records vehicle information including maximum load capacity.

📑 Compulsory liability insurance certificate 自賠責保険証

Must be kept in the vehicle. Driving without valid insurance (無保険運転) carries very severe penalties.

🚨 Hazard warning device 停止表示器材

Mandatory on expressways (e.g. a warning triangle). In the event of a breakdown, it must be placed at least 50 m behind the vehicle.

14-3　Safety Protective Equipment

Equipment

Who Must Use It

Exceptions

🪑 Seatbelt シートベルト

Driver and all passengers (including rear seats)

Limited exceptions: emergency medical transport, pregnant passengers where wearing is medically inadvisable, police on duty, etc.

👶 Child safety seat チャイルドシート

Children aged 6 and under

Limited exceptions: emergency medical transport, bus/taxi passengers, children whose physique makes fitting the seat impractical, etc.

🪖 Helmet ヘルメット

Riders and passengers on motorcycles and mopeds

Virtually no exceptions — helmets are mandatory.

💡 Child seat placement — a common misconception

"A young child is safer in the front seat because the driver can keep an eye on them" — this is wrong. The front seat has an airbag, which is actually dangerous for young children. Children aged 6 and under should in principle ride in the rear seat in a child safety seat.

14-4　Statutory Duties When an Accident Occurs

Hit-and-run (轢き逃げ) carries extremely heavy penalties. The legally required sequence of actions after an accident is as follows:

Step

Duty

Details

1

Stop immediately

Bring the vehicle to a stop at once. All parties involved in an accident have a duty not to leave the scene.

2

Assist the injured

If there are injured persons, call an ambulance (119) immediately and provide assistance. Even if the other party was at fault, you still have a duty to render aid.

3

Prevent further danger

Move the vehicle to a safe location, set out warning triangles, and direct other vehicles around the scene to prevent secondary accidents.

4

Report to police

Call 110 to notify the police. Even if the other party says "no need", you must still report — this is a statutory duty.

5

Wait for police to arrive

Do not leave the scene. Exchange details with all parties (licence, licence plate, insurance), and only leave after the police have confirmed everything.

⚠️ Important

"The other party said not to call the police." "We agreed to settle it privately." "I was in a hurry so I left." — none of these are acceptable. Leaving the scene without reporting to police or providing assistance constitutes both 救護義務違反 (failure to render aid) and 報告義務違反 (failure to report), which carry far heavier penalties than a standard traffic offence.

14-5　Licence Penalty Points System

Japan uses a demerit-points system — violations accumulate points, and once a certain threshold is reached your licence is suspended or revoked.

Violation Type

Points (typical examples)

Drink-driving (breath alcohol ≥ 0.25 mg/L)

25 pts (immediate revocation)

Drunk driving (酒酔い運転)

35 pts (immediate revocation)

Hit-and-run (轢き逃げ)

35 pts (immediate revocation)

Speeding 50 km/h or more above the limit

12 pts

Speeding 30–50 km/h above the limit

6 pts

Illegal parking or stopping violations

1–3 pts

Mobile phone use / operation while driving

3 pts

Seatbelt not worn

1 pt

Note: Points are adjusted based on the severity of the violation, whether it is a repeat offence, and whether personal injury resulted. Refer to the National Police Agency's official announcements for current figures.

14-6　Common Exam Trap Questions

Question

Answer

Explanation

I drank alcohol the night before, but after sleeping it off I can drive safely.

✗

Alcohol metabolism varies from person to person. It may take far longer than a night's sleep for blood alcohol to reach zero.

Cold medicine may contain ingredients that cause drowsiness, so you should not drive after taking it.

○

Driving under the influence of drugs that impair driving ability is one of the absolutely prohibited situations.

It is fine to take a call while driving using a hands-free device or Bluetooth earpiece.

✗

Even hands-free, prolonged phone conversations while driving constitutes distracted driving. Except in emergencies, you should pull over to the side of the road.

A young child is safer sitting in the front seat because the driver can see them.

✗

The front seat has an airbag, which is actually dangerous for young children. They should ride in the rear seat in a child safety seat.

For a minor scrape, both parties can agree not to call the police and simply leave.

✗

Reporting to police after an accident is a statutory duty. You cannot settle privately and simply leave.

You know the other person has just been drinking, but you get in their car anyway because you want to get home early.

✗

Knowingly riding as a passenger in a drink-driver's vehicle is also a criminal offence.
