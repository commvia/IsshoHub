Chapter 7 — Licence Types & Eligible Vehicles

Once you understand the vehicle categories, the next step is licences. Each licence covers a different range of vehicles — below we cover both the eligibility requirements and the permitted driving scope.

7-1　Eligibility Requirements for Four-Wheel Licences

From standard to large, there are four tiers based on age and prior experience. Note that the 中型 (medium) and 大型 (large) licences require you to already hold a 普通免許 (standard licence) for a minimum number of years.

📌 Key Points

🔸 Four-wheel hierarchy:　Large ＞ Medium ＞ Semi-Medium ＞ Standard. A higher-tier licence automatically includes all lower tiers.

🔸 Motorbike hierarchy:　Large Motorbike ＞ Standard Motorbike. However, motorbike licences do not permit driving four-wheel vehicles, and vice versa.

🔸 Universal add-ons:　Most licences automatically include the right to drive 小型特殊 (small special) vehicles and 原付 (mopeds) — the only exceptions are the 小型特殊免許, 原付免許, and 牽引免許 themselves.

🔸 Towing is separate:　Towing a trailer requires a standalone 牽引免許 (towing licence), which must be held in addition to a licence for the towing vehicle itself.

🔸 Large Special is not interchangeable with ordinary cars:　The 大型特殊免許 covers special-purpose machinery (e.g. bulldozers, forklifts) but does not permit driving ordinary four-wheel motor vehicles; conversely, ordinary four-wheel licences do not cover large special vehicles.

7-3　Quick-Recall Points

🧠 One-sentence summary

"Higher licences cover lower tiers downward; motorbike / four-wheel / special / towing categories are entirely separate; most licences include 小型特殊 and 原付 as a bonus."

❓ Common exam questions

・Holding a 普通免許 — can you drive a 大型特殊 vehicle? ❌ No (different category system)

・Holding a 普通免許 — can you ride a moped (原付)? ✅ Yes (普通免許 includes 原付)

・Holding a 大型二輪免許 — can you drive an ordinary car? ❌ No (motorbike licences do not cover four-wheel vehicles)
